# Caffeine for the prevention of injuries and errors in shift workers - PMC

*Source*: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4160007/
*Fetched*: 2026-03-10T02:08:50.865883Z

Jet lag and shift work disorder are circadian rhythm sleep disorders which result in sleepiness and can elevate injury risk. They create a need for individuals to operate at times which are different to those dictated by their circadian rhythms. Consequently there is also a need for interventions to help ensure that these persons can do so safely. Caffeine has a potential role in promoting alertness during times of desired wakefulness in persons with jet lag or shift work disorder, however its effects on injury and error are unclear.
